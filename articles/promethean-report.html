The Promethean Report: British Empire, Deep State & The Hidden Hand

A political movement claims the British Empire never fell — it just went underground. Their dossiers allege royal involvement in presidential assassinations and a secret war against American sovereignty.

February 13, 2026 • 12 min read • Deep State • Hidden History
What if the American Revolution never truly ended? What if the British Empire — supposedly dismantled after World War II — simply transformed into something more insidious: a shadow network pulling strings behind the scenes of world events?

That's the central claim of Promethean Action, a political organization that emerged from the intellectual legacy of Lyndon LaRouche. Their reports, dossiers, and publications paint a picture of global conspiracy that makes most theories look tame by comparison.

The Core Claim: Britain Never Let Go

According to Promethean Action, the British Empire didn't collapse — it evolved. Their theory posits that British financial interests, centered around the City of London, continue to exercise enormous control over global affairs through:

  • Central banking systems — including alleged influence over the Federal Reserve
  • Intelligence networks — particularly the "Five Eyes" alliance
  • Media manipulation — controlling narratives through global news networks
  • Drug trade — echoing historical opium wars
  • Targeted assassinations — including American presidents

The Dossier: "It Is The British Who Murder Our Presidents"

In February 2024, Promethean Action published what they call a "Police Dossier" alleging British involvement in the assassinations of Abraham Lincoln, William McKinley, and John F. Kennedy. The document claims to trace a pattern of British intelligence operations designed to eliminate American leaders who challenged imperial financial interests.

Who Is Promethean Action?

Promethean Action launched publicly in 2024 as a rebranded successor to LaRouche PAC. The organization traces its intellectual lineage to Lyndon LaRouche (1922-2019), a controversial political figure who ran for president eight times and developed an elaborate worldview combining:

  • Classical economics and opposition to monetarism
  • Support for nuclear fusion and space colonization
  • Conspiracy theories about British imperialism
  • Claims of assassination attempts against himself

LaRouche was a divisive figure. Supporters viewed him as a visionary; critics called him a cult leader. He served time in federal prison on fraud charges — which his followers claim were politically motivated.

The "Four Powers Agreement" Theory

One of Promethean Action's key proposals is LaRouche's "Four Powers Agreement" — a geopolitical alliance between the United States, Russia, China, and India that they claim would break the British financial empire's grip on the world.

According to this theory, these four nations working together could:

  • Replace the current dollar-based reserve system
  • End speculative finance and return to productive economics
  • Launch massive infrastructure projects worldwide
  • Prevent World War III (which they claim British interests are engineering)

Important Context

Media Bias/Fact Check rates Promethean Action as "far-right biased" and "questionable" based on promotion of conspiracy theories and poor sourcing. However, mainstream dismissal doesn't necessarily mean every claim is false — it means independent verification is essential.

What Can Be Verified?

Let's separate verifiable facts from the more speculative claims:

✓ Verifiable

  • The City of London operates as a separate financial jurisdiction with unique legal status
  • The Five Eyes intelligence alliance (US, UK, Canada, Australia, New Zealand) does exist
  • Historical British involvement in opium trade and colonial exploitation is documented
  • British intelligence has conducted operations on American soil (documented cases)

? Speculative

  • Claims of British orchestration of presidential assassinations
  • Allegations of Queen Elizabeth II ordering hits on LaRouche
  • The idea that British interests control the Federal Reserve
  • Claims of a unified "British Imperial" agenda coordinating global events

Why This Matters

Whether you believe Promethean Action's claims or dismiss them entirely, they represent a significant strand of American political thought that has persisted for decades. Their ideas have influenced:

  • Economic populism and criticism of Wall Street
  • Support for industrial policy and manufacturing
  • Skepticism of "free trade" agreements
  • Advocacy for space exploration and nuclear energy

In 2024-2025, Promethean Action aligned strongly with the MAGA movement, organizing at state GOP conventions and promoting Trump's "Agenda 47" policies. They claim their ground-game efforts contributed to Trump's election victory.
