THE QUICK VERSION

In 2013, Edward Snowden leaked thousands of NSA documents proving what "conspiracy theorists" had warned about for years: mass surveillance of American citizens without warrants. Programs like PRISM gave the NSA direct access to Google, Facebook, Apple, and Microsoft servers. XKeyscore lets analysts search virtually anything anyone does online. The government collects and stores your metadata — who you call, when, for how long, where you were. Courts have since ruled some programs unconstitutional, but the infrastructure remains. And now commercial spyware like Pegasus gives governments the ability to turn your phone into a full surveillance device — camera, mic, everything.

KNOWN SURVEILLANCE PROGRAMS

NSA Snowden Leak

PRISM

Direct access to servers of Google, Apple, Facebook, Microsoft, Yahoo, and others. Collects emails, chats, videos, photos, file transfers, VoIP calls. Started in 2007. Tech companies initially denied knowledge.

Guardian Report
NSA Snowden Leak

XKeyscore

Search engine for virtually everything: emails, browsing history, chats, metadata. NSA analysts could search without authorization or warrant. "Nearly everything a typical user does on the internet."

Guardian Report
NSA Section 215

Bulk Phone Metadata Collection

Every phone call made in America: who called whom, when, how long, from where. Stored for years. Court ruled unconstitutional in 2015, but collection continued under other authorities.

ACLU Analysis
PRIVATE NSO Group

Pegasus Spyware

Israeli spyware sold to governments. Zero-click exploit — no user action needed. Full access: camera, microphone, messages, location, passwords. Used against journalists, activists, politicians. Your phone is compromised and you'll never know.

Amnesty Report
FBI Domestic

COINTELPRO (Historical)

FBI's domestic surveillance program (1956-1971). Targeted civil rights leaders, anti-war activists, Black Panthers. Wiretaps, infiltration, blackmail. MLK received anonymous FBI letter urging suicide. "Officially" ended, but similar tactics continue.

FBI Vault
ALLIANCE International

Five Eyes

US, UK, Canada, Australia, New Zealand share intelligence. Allows bypassing domestic surveillance laws: UK spies on Americans, shares with NSA. NSA spies on Brits, shares with GCHQ. Legal loophole for global surveillance.

EFF Report
